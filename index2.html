Cat Coat Patterns

  • Solid color coats: These are the easiest type of fur to spot because there’s no variation in colors. The most common solid color coats in cats are black, red, cream, chocolate, lilac, fawn, cinnamon, blue, and white.
  • Tabby coats: Any cat with a distinctive ‘M’ shaped marking on its forehead, stripes around its eyes and across its cheeks, along its back, and around its legs and tail, and distinctive striped, lined, swirled, dotted, banded, or flecked patterns on the body neck, sides, shoulders, chest, flanks, as well as abdomen is referred to as a tabby cat.
  • Tortoiseshell coats: Tortoiseshell cats have a coat that is a mix of black, orange, and sometimes white.
  • Bicolor coats: Bicolor cats have a coat that is made up of two colors, usually black and white.
  • Colorpoint coats: Colorpoint cats have a coat that is lighter in color on their body and darker on their face, ears, feet, and tail.
  • Tricolor coats: Tricolor cats have a coat that is made up of three colors, usually black, orange, and white.

If ever you see a cat and can not able to able identify it here are some examples of the most common cats you can find:


British Shorthair


  • Appearance: The British Shorthair has a broad chest, muscular neck, and a strong jaws. The legs are thick and strong, and the tail is of medium length with a thick base and a slight taper that leads to a well-rounded tip. The coat is short and very dense, and the eyes are large and round.

  • Personality: British Shorthairs are affectionate, easy-going, and calm. They are great family cats and get along well with children and other pets. They are active without being hyper and can live happily in almost any size house, including apartments. They are fiercely loyal and will attach themselves to every one of their family members.

  • Care: British Shorthairs require little grooming, but they enjoy spending time being brushed. They are prone to obesity, so it’s important to monitor their diet and exercise. They are also prone to certain health problems, such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.

Maine Coon


  • Appearance: The Maine Coon has a broad chest, muscular neck, and strong jaws. The legs are thick and strong, and the tail is of medium length with a thick base and a slight taper that leads to a well-rounded tip. The coat is short and very dense, and the eyes are large and round.

  • Personality: Maine Coons are affectionate, gentle, and sociable. They are great family pets and get along well with children and other animals. They are also known for their hunting skills and were originally used to protect food stores from rodents.

  • Care: Maine Coons require regular grooming to keep their long, fluffy fur in good condition. They are prone to certain health problems, such as hip dysplasia and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.

Persian


  • Appearance: The Persian cat has a round face, short muzzle, and large, round eyes. The breed is known for its long silky fur. Persian cats come in a variety of colors and patterns, including solid, bicolor, tabby, tortoiseshell, and more.

  • Personality: Persian cats are affectionate and docile. They are generally quiet and make great pets for apartment living.

  • Care: Persian cats require regular grooming to prevent matting of their long, silky fur. They are prone to certain health problems, such as polycystic kidney disease and respiratory issues, so regular check-ups with a veterinarian are recommended.

Siamese


  • Appearance: The Siamese cat has a broad chest, muscular neck, strong jaws, and a well-developed muzzle. The legs are thick and strong, and the tail is of medium length with a thick base and a slight taper that leads to a well-rounded tip. The coat is short and very dense, and the eyes are large and round.

  • Personality: Siamese cats are highly intelligent, playful, and affectionate. They are also known for their vocal nature and will “talk” to their owners frequently. They are highly social and require constant interaction with their owners or other pets. Siamese cats are also known for their even-keeled temperaments and blend well into families with children and other pets.

  • Care: Siamese cats require little grooming, but they enjoy spending time being brushed. They are prone to certain health problems, such as respiratory issues and dental problems.
